Domestic Hate Crime Bias Motivations(2005 - 2015)

The majority of hate crime incidents are the result of race, religion or sexual orientation bias. Disability bias hate crimes and other bias hate crimes are tracked by the department of Justice but they are few in number, averaging less than 100 incidents per year.

Race bias hate crimes occur three times more frequently than either religion or sexual orientation bias hate crimes. Race bias and religion bias hate crimes dropped during the first six years of Barack Obamaâ€™s presidency. It is interesting to note that during this same time period sexual orientation hate crime rate remained static.

2015 saw an increase of 3%, 4% and 22% for hates crimes involving race bias, sexual orientation bias and religion bias, respectively. The significant increase in religion bias hate crimes may be attributed to overall increase in domestic anti-Islamic sentiment since 2013 and specific acts of domestic terrorism, such as the mass shootings in San Bernardino, California conducted by two persons who pledged allegiance to the Islamic State. This increase may also be attributed to the divisive presidential campaign, which began in earnest in 2015.
