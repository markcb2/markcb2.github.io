End of the guided Journey; but there is still more to explore on your own

So far you have been taken on a guided journey into two major dimensions of domestic hate crime: the motivation behind the crime and the demographics of the offenders. . We have learned that hate crimes motivated by racial prejudice are still a major blemish in the American fabric. We have also learned that hates crimes due to religious or sexual orientation bias are also still a significant occurrence. We also know that while whites commit the most hate crimes they commit fewer hate crimes relative to their representation in the U.S. population as a whole. We also know that as a group, Hispanics and Latinos commit hate crimes at a lower proportion to their representation in the the U.S. population as a whole. Finally, we learned that the second leading hate crime offender group are unidentified perpetrators . As such, offender demographics are directional.

What is missing from this presentation so far is the connection between the types of hate crimes and the offenders that commit these crimes. The next page is a chance for you to explore these dimensions in further detail. It should be noted that in the interest of time only two of the three dominant hate crime bias motivations -- race bias and religion bias -- are available for self-exploration. There are some surprises -- especially if you take a deeper dive into anti-black hate based crimes and the perpetrators that commit these crimes.
